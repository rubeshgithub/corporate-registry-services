Alberta corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Alberta)* (ABCA) must file an annual return every year with the Alberta Corporate Registry to maintain their good standing. Alberta has one of the shortest filing windows in Canada — annual returns are due within just one month of the corporation's anniversary date.

## What Is an Alberta Annual Return?

An Alberta annual return is a statutory filing that confirms your corporation's continued existence, updates director and address information on the registry, and keeps your corporation in active good standing under Alberta law.

## When Is the Alberta Annual Return Due?

Alberta annual returns are due **within 1 month of your corporation's anniversary date**. The anniversary date is the date the corporation was originally incorporated. This is a strict deadline — Alberta's 1-month window is the shortest of any Canadian jurisdiction.

For example: if your corporation was incorporated on July 22, your annual return is due by August 22 every year.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Alberta)* must file, including:
- Active operating corporations
- Holding companies
- Non-operating or dormant corporations
- Corporations with no employees and no revenue

## What Information Is Required?

An Alberta annual return requires:

- Full legal name of the corporation
- Alberta Corporate Access Number (CAN)
- Registered office and records address (must be in Alberta)
- Names and addresses of all current directors and officers
- Designated officer designation (if applicable)

## How to File

Alberta annual returns are filed through the **Alberta Corporate Registry (CORES)** portal, accessible through [alberta.ca](https://www.alberta.ca/annual-return-information.aspx).

**Steps:**
1. Access CORES Online (Corporate Registry Online filing)
2. Search for your corporation using your Corporate Access Number
3. Select "File Annual Return" from your corporation's profile
4. Confirm or update director and address information
5. Pay the $47 government filing fee
6. Receive confirmation of filing

## Government Fee

The Alberta government fee for an annual return is **$47**, payable online through CORES.

## Alberta's Strict 1-Month Deadline

The 1-month deadline is often overlooked by Alberta corporations, particularly those that have relocated their registered office or changed directors without updating the registry. Since CRS monitors your anniversary date and files well in advance of the deadline, you eliminate the risk of late filing entirely.

## Director Residency Requirements

Alberta corporations have **no Canadian residency requirement for directors**. All directors may be non-Canadian residents, making Alberta a popular jurisdiction for incorporation by international entrepreneurs.

## Consequences of Missing the Deadline

Alberta is strict about annual return compliance. Missing the deadline results in:

1. The corporation entering **non-compliance** status on the registry
2. A notice from the registry warning of potential dissolution
3. Administrative dissolution if the filing remains outstanding
4. A revival application required to restore the corporation (additional fees and processing time apply)
