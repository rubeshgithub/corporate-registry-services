Every corporation incorporated under British Columbia's *Business Corporations Act* (BCA) must file an annual report each year to maintain its good standing with BC Registry Services. In British Columbia, the annual filing is called an **Annual Report** (not an "annual return" as in other provinces), but the requirement and purpose are the same.

## What Is a BC Annual Report?

A BC Annual Report is a statutory filing that confirms your corporation is still active, updates the registry on your directors and registered address, and keeps your corporation in good standing under BC law.

## When Is the BC Annual Report Due?

The BC Annual Report is due **within 2 months of your corporation's anniversary date** (the date of incorporation). For example, if your corporation was incorporated on April 10, your annual report is due by June 10 every year.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the BC *Business Corporations Act* must file annually:
- Active business corporations
- Non-reporting (private) companies
- Holding companies
- Dormant or inactive corporations

There is no exception for corporations with no activity. Even a corporation that did nothing during the year must file.

## What Information Is Required?

The BC Annual Report requires:

- Full legal name of the corporation
- BC Incorporation Number
- Registered office and records office address (must be a physical BC address)
- Central securities register location
- Names, addresses, and occupations of all current directors
- Prescribed information about share structure (if any changes occurred)

## How to File

BC Annual Reports are filed through **BC Registry Services** at [bcregistry.gov.bc.ca](https://www.bcregistry.gov.bc.ca).

**Steps:**
1. Log in to BC Registry Services using a BC Services Card or BCeID
2. Search for and access your company's dashboard
3. Select "File Annual Report" when the filing becomes due
4. Confirm or update your company's information (directors, registered office)
5. Review and submit
6. Pay the $43 government filing fee

## Government Fee

The BC government fee for an annual report is **$43**, payable online through the BC Registry Services portal.

## Consequences of Not Filing

If the BC Annual Report is not filed within the 2-month period:

1. BC Registry Services will send a reminder
2. If continued non-compliance, the corporation is placed in **non-compliance** status
3. If the annual report is more than one year overdue, the corporation may be **dissolved** by the Registrar
4. Once dissolved, the corporation must apply for reinstatement, which involves additional government fees and processing time

## Directors and Residency Requirements

Unlike Ontario and federal corporations, BC corporations have **no Canadian residency requirement for directors**. This makes BC a popular incorporation jurisdiction for entrepreneurs and investors who are not Canadian residents.
