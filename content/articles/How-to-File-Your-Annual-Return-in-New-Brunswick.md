New Brunswick corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (New Brunswick)* must file an annual return with Service New Brunswick every year. New Brunswick operates on a combined anniversary-and-calendar system for its annual return deadlines.

## When Is the New Brunswick Annual Return Due?

New Brunswick annual returns are due **by February 1 each year**, or within 30 days of the corporation's anniversary date, whichever is earlier.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (New Brunswick)*, including extra-provincial corporations registered in New Brunswick, must file annually — including dormant corporations.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and New Brunswick Corporation Number
- Registered office address in New Brunswick
- Names and addresses of all current directors and officers
- Date of the most recent annual general meeting

## How to File

New Brunswick annual returns are filed through **Service New Brunswick** at [snb.ca](https://www.snb.ca).

**Steps:**
1. Access the Service New Brunswick business portal
2. Search for and access your corporation's filing
3. Update corporation information as required
4. Pay the $50 government fee
5. Receive confirmation

## Government Fee

The New Brunswick government fee is **$50**.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to file results in the corporation being placed in non-compliance, and ultimately administrative dissolution if unresolved. Revival requires a separate process.
