Newfoundland and Labrador corporations incorporated under the *Corporations Act (Newfoundland and Labrador)* must file an annual return with the Registry of Companies within 3 months of the corporation's anniversary date.

## When Is the Newfoundland Annual Return Due?

Newfoundland annual returns are due **within 3 months of the corporation's anniversary date** (the date of incorporation).

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Corporations Act (Newfoundland and Labrador)*, plus extra-provincial corporations registered in the province, must file annually — including inactive corporations.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and Newfoundland Corporation Number
- Registered office address in Newfoundland and Labrador
- Names and addresses of current directors and officers
- Principal business activities

## How to File

Newfoundland annual returns are filed through the **Registry of Companies** at [digital.gov.nl.ca](https://www.digital.gov.nl.ca/corporate/registry).

**Steps:**
1. Access the Registry of Companies portal
2. Locate your corporation
3. File the annual return and confirm or update information
4. Pay the $100 government fee
5. Receive confirmation of filing

## Government Fee

The Newfoundland and Labrador government fee is **$100** — the highest of any Canadian jurisdiction, reflecting the province's registry cost structure.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Failure to file results in non-compliance status, followed by notice of intent to dissolve, and ultimately administrative dissolution. Revival requires a formal application and fees.
