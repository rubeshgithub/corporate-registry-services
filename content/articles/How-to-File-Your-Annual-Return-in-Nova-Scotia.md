Nova Scotia corporations incorporated under the *Companies Act (Nova Scotia)* or *Corporations Registration Act* must file an annual return with the Registry of Joint Stock Companies every year within 30 days of the corporation's anniversary date — one of the tightest deadlines in Canada.

## When Is the Nova Scotia Annual Return Due?

Nova Scotia annual returns are due **within 30 days of the corporation's anniversary date**. The 30-day window is strict, making timely action essential. If your corporation was incorporated on September 5, your annual return is due by October 5 each year.

## Who Must File?

All corporations registered with the Nova Scotia Registry of Joint Stock Companies, including extra-provincial corporations carrying on business in Nova Scotia.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and Nova Scotia Registry Number
- Registered office address in Nova Scotia
- Names and addresses of current directors and officers
- Principal business activities

## How to File

Nova Scotia annual returns are filed through the **Registry of Joint Stock Companies** at [novascotia.ca/sns/access/business](https://www.novascotia.ca/sns/access/business/registry-of-joint-stock-companies/).

**Steps:**
1. Access the Registry of Joint Stock Companies portal
2. Search for your corporation
3. File the annual return and update corporation information
4. Pay the $25 government fee
5. Receive confirmation

## Government Fee

The Nova Scotia government fee is **$25**.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Given the strict 30-day window, administrative dissolution can follow relatively quickly after a missed annual return. Reinstatement requires a formal application, additional fees, and processing time.
