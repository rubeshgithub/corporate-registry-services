Corporations incorporated in Nunavut under the *Business Corporations Act (Nunavut)* must file an annual return with the Nunavut Department of Justice Corporate Registry within 2 months of the corporation's anniversary date.

## When Is the Nunavut Annual Return Due?

Nunavut annual returns are due **within 2 months of the corporation's anniversary date**.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Nunavut)*, plus extra-provincial corporations registered in Nunavut.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and Nunavut Corporation Number
- Registered office address in Nunavut
- Names and addresses of all current directors and officers

## How to File

Nunavut annual returns are filed through the **Nunavut Department of Justice Corporate Registry**. Given Nunavut's remote nature, many corporations use a registered agent or service provider (like CRS) to handle their filings.

**Steps:**
1. Contact the Nunavut Corporate Registry or use the online portal
2. File the annual return with current corporation information
3. Pay the $50 government fee
4. Receive confirmation

## Government Fee

The Nunavut government fee is **$50**.
