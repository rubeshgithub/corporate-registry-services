Every Ontario corporation incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Ontario)* (OBCA) must file an annual return each year to remain in good standing with the Ontario Business Registry. Missing this filing can result in a notice of intent to dissolve — and ultimately, administrative dissolution of your corporation.

## What Is an Ontario Annual Return?

An Ontario annual return is a statutory filing that confirms your corporation is still active, updates the registry with any changes to your directors or registered address, and maintains your corporation's good standing under Ontario law. It is separate from your tax return filed with the Canada Revenue Agency.

## When Is the Ontario Annual Return Due?

Ontario annual returns are due **within 6 months of your corporation's fiscal year-end**. For example, if your fiscal year ends December 31, your annual return is due by June 30 of the following year.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Ontario Business Corporations Act* must file annually, including:
- Active corporations carrying on business
- Holding corporations
- Non-operating or dormant corporations
- Corporations in the process of winding up

Even if your corporation had no revenue, no employees, and no activity in the year, the annual return must still be filed.

## What Information Is Required?

The Ontario annual return requires:

- Full legal name of the corporation
- Ontario Corporation Number (OCN)
- Registered office address in Ontario
- Principal place of business (if different)
- Names and addresses of all current directors
- Names and addresses of all current officers
- Date of the last annual general meeting or written resolution

## How to File

Ontario annual returns are filed through the **Ontario Business Registry** at [Ontario.ca/businessregistry](https://www.ontario.ca/page/ontario-business-registry).

**Steps:**
1. Log in or create an account on the Ontario Business Registry
2. Search for your corporation using your Ontario Corporation Number
3. Claim or confirm access to your corporation's profile
4. Select "File Annual Return" from the dashboard
5. Review and update your corporation's information
6. Pay the $22 government filing fee (online payment)
7. Receive confirmation of filing

## Ontario Annual Return Government Fee

The Ontario government fee for filing an annual return is **$22** (filed online). This fee is payable to the province regardless of whether you file yourself or use a service provider.

## What Happens If You Miss the Filing?

If you do not file your Ontario annual return within the required period, the Ontario Business Registry will:

1. Send a notice of failure to comply
2. Issue a notice of intent to dissolve
3. Administratively dissolve the corporation if no response is received

A dissolved Ontario corporation can be **revived** under Section 241 of the OBCA, but revival requires a separate application, additional fees, and can take weeks to process. The cost and delay of a revival far outweigh the cost of filing the annual return on time.

## Extra-Provincial Annual Returns

If your Ontario corporation is also registered as an extra-provincial corporation in other provinces — for example, British Columbia or Alberta — you must file separate annual returns in each of those provinces as well. Each province has its own deadlines and fees.
