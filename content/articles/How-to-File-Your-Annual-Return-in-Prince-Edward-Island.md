Prince Edward Island corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (PEI)* must file an annual return with the PEI Corporate Registry within 30 days of the corporation's anniversary date.

## When Is the PEI Annual Return Due?

PEI annual returns are due **within 30 days of the corporation's anniversary date**. This is one of the tighter deadlines in Canada — PEI corporations must act quickly after their anniversary date each year.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (PEI)*, plus extra-provincial corporations registered in Prince Edward Island.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and PEI Corporation Number
- Registered office address in PEI
- Names and addresses of current directors and officers
- Date of most recent annual general meeting

## How to File

PEI annual returns are filed through the **PEI Corporate Registry** at [princeedwardisland.ca](https://www.princeedwardisland.ca/en/topic/corporate-registry).

**Steps:**
1. Access the PEI Corporate Registry portal
2. Locate your corporation
3. File the annual return, updating information as needed
4. Pay the $50 government fee
5. Receive confirmation

## Government Fee

The PEI government fee is **$50**.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Missing the 30-day window results in non-compliance status. Continued non-filing can lead to administrative dissolution. Revival requires an application and additional fees.
