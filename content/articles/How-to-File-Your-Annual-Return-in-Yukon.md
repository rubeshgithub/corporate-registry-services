Yukon corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Yukon)* must file an annual return with Corporate Affairs Yukon within 1 month of the corporation's anniversary date — one of the shortest deadlines in Canada alongside Alberta.

## When Is the Yukon Annual Return Due?

Yukon annual returns are due **within 1 month of the corporation's anniversary date**. This is a very tight window that requires proactive management of your filing calendar.

## Who Must File?

All corporations incorporated under the *Business Corporations Act (Yukon)*, plus extra-provincial corporations registered in Yukon.

## What Information Is Required?

- Full legal name and Yukon Corporation Number
- Registered office address in Yukon
- Names and addresses of all current directors and officers

## How to File

Yukon annual returns are filed through **Corporate Affairs Yukon** at [gov.yk.ca/corporate-affairs](https://yukon.ca/en/doing-business/registering-business/annual-return).

**Steps:**
1. Access the Corporate Affairs Yukon portal
2. Locate your corporation
3. File the annual return and update information
4. Pay the $50 government fee
5. Receive confirmation

## Government Fee

The Yukon government fee is **$50**.

## Director Residency Requirements

Yukon corporations have no specific Canadian residency requirement for directors beyond general CBCA-equivalent provisions, making Yukon attractive for international incorporations.
