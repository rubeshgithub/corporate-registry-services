# CRS Canada - Certificate of Good Standing / Certificate of Status [Source](https://CRS/Canada-certificate-of-good-standing.html) 

What is a Certificate of Good Standing? It’s an official certificate issued by the federal or provincial corporate registry confirming: The corporation legally exists under its governing Act Required filings and fees are up to date (e.g., annual returns) The corporation is active and not dissolved, amalgamated, or discontinued Jurisdictions may title it “Certificate of Status,” “Certificate of Compliance,” or “Good Standing”

Why it matters. Banks, regulators, investors, and counterparties often require an official certificate as proof of a company’s current legal standing — for financing, account opening, extra-provincial registrations, tenders, M&A, and due diligence. Unlike a Corporate Profile Report (detailed registry data), a Certificate of Good Standing provides formal certification of legal status.
