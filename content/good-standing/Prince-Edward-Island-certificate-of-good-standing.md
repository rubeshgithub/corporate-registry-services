A Prince Edward Island Certificate of Good Standing — also known as a Certificate of Status — is an official document issued by the PEI Corporate Registry confirming that a corporation is legally registered, active, and compliant with all filing requirements under the Prince Edward Island Business Corporations Act.

This certificate is formal proof that a company is in good legal standing and remains authorized to operate. It is commonly required for banking, financing, extra-provincial registration, mergers, acquisitions, and official government or investor due diligence.

The certificate confirms:

- The corporation legally exists and is duly registered
- The corporation is active and has not been dissolved, discontinued, or amalgamated
- All required filings and annual returns are current and up to date
- The company is in good standing with the PEI Corporate Registry

The Prince Edward Island Certificate of Good Standing is retrieved directly from the PEI Corporate Registry, ensuring authenticity and accuracy.
