Two of the most commonly requested corporate documents in Canada are the **Corporate Profile Report** and the **Certificate of Good Standing**. They sound similar — both come from the provincial or federal corporate registry, both confirm facts about a corporation — but they serve different purposes and contain different information. Knowing which one you need can save time, money, and frustration.

## The Quick Answer

- **Corporate Profile Report** — a comprehensive snapshot of a corporation's public record, including directors, registered address, corporate history, and filing status. Use it when you need detailed, verifiable information about a specific company.
- **Certificate of Good Standing** — a brief official certification that a corporation is actively registered and compliant with all filing requirements. Use it when you need to prove your own corporation is in good standing for a third party.

## What Is a Corporate Profile Report?

A Corporate Profile Report (also called a Corporate Search or Corporate Extract in some provinces) is a full extract of a corporation's public record from the corporate registry. It is a **research document** — used when you want to find out facts about a company.

Common contents include:

- Current and historical legal names (including all name changes)
- Corporate registry number
- Date of incorporation or registration
- Registered and records office address
- Names and addresses of all current directors and officers
- Current legal status (active, dissolved, amalgamated, etc.)
- Date of most recent annual return filing
- Share capital and authorized capital (in some provinces)
- Extra-provincial registration details (if applicable)

**Who requests Corporate Profile Reports:**
- Accountants verifying a client's company for QuickBooks Online or payroll setup
- Lawyers conducting due diligence for M&A transactions
- Banks completing Know Your Customer (KYC) checks on business clients
- Businesses verifying a supplier, vendor, or joint venture partner
- Individuals checking if a business name is legitimately registered

## What Is a Certificate of Good Standing?

A Certificate of Good Standing (also called a Certificate of Status, Certificate of Compliance, or Certificate of Existence depending on the province) is a **compliance certificate** — used when your own corporation needs to prove its good standing to a third party.

Unlike a profile report, it is not a detailed informational document. It is a short, official certificate that says, in effect: "This corporation is legally registered, has met all its filing obligations, and is in good standing as of today."

**Who requests Certificates of Good Standing:**
- Corporations registering in a new province as an extra-provincial corporation
- Business owners applying for bank financing or a line of credit
- Corporations tendering for government contracts
- Companies being acquired (sellers provide this during M&A due diligence)
- Corporations licensing or franchising their business
- Lawyers and notaries completing real estate closings involving corporate parties

## Can One Document Substitute for the Other?

Generally, no. The documents serve different purposes and requesting parties will specify which one they need.

A bank requiring a Certificate of Good Standing for a mortgage application will not accept a Corporate Profile Report in its place — even though the profile report contains more information and also confirms the corporation's status.

Conversely, an accountant requesting a Corporate Profile Report to verify directors' information for QuickBooks does not need a Certificate of Good Standing, which would not provide the director details they need.

When in doubt, ask the requesting party specifically which document they require.
